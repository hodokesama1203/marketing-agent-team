## Evidence rules used in this brief

- `verified`: directly supported by an allowed official product source.
- `user-supplied`: supplied in the campaign state or task instructions.
- `hypothesis`: an audience, search, objection, message-framing, or conversion assumption that requires the stated validation method.
- A verified UFB characteristic is not treated as a guaranteed outcome in a particular field. The official site itself says actual effects and measurements vary with gas, water quality, generation method, and other application conditions `[verified: S3]`.
- Industrial model selection is not inferred. The official site asks for flow rate, pipe specification, and intended use before proposing a model/application method `[verified: S2]`.
- This agent team conducts no interviews, outreach, customer follow-up, customer contact, or material delivery. Validation may use only inbound inquiry records, user-supplied pre-existing research/field-test data, and other permitted read-only evidence.

## Verified foundation

1. i-UFB is IMB’s brand for UFB-generation nozzles, engines, and application systems `[verified: S1, S2, S4]`.
2. The official site describes UFB as bubbles smaller than 1 µm and identifies water dispersion, gas-liquid contact, contaminant penetration/adsorption, and possible reactive-species generation as general characteristics; actual results vary by conditions `[verified: S3]`.
3. The official site presents agriculture, livestock, aquaculture, industrial cleaning, sterilization processes, and water-quality improvement as application areas `[verified: S1, S2]`.
4. The industrial lineup includes five engine names and BN/SUS316L nozzle families; exact fit depends on field inputs and is not established in this brief `[verified: S2]`.
5. The consumer contexts are FW24 for a 24 mm washbasin fitting, FWP6A as an inline water-purifier type, and Bubblio as a UFB shower `[verified: S1, S2]`.
